Ada Lovelace

The World's First Computer Programmer

Timeline of an Extraordinary Mind

  • 1815 - Born in London, England, to the famous romantic poet Lord Byron and Lady Byron.
  • 1833 - Met Charles Babbage, a renowned mathematician, through their mutual friend Mary Somerville. She became fascinated by his plans for the Difference Engine.
  • 1842 - Translated an article by Italian military engineer Luigi Menabrea on Babbage’s newest machine design: the Analytical Engine.
  • 1843 - Published her translated work along with extensive notes. Her "Notes," labeled A through G, ended up being longer than the original article itself.
  • Legacy - Note G contained an algorithm designed to compute Bernoulli numbers. This step-by-step sequence of operations is universally recognized today as the world's very first computer program.

"The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns just as the Jacquard loom weaves flowers and leaves."

— Ada Lovelace
